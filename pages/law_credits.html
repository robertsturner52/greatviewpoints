THE SUMMIT OF TELESCOPE PEAK HAS ONE OF MY FAVORITE VIEWS IN ALL OF THE AMERICAN WEST. AT 11,043 FEET ELEVATION, THE MOUNTAIN TOWERS OVER DEATH VALLEY DIRECTLY ABOVE NORTH AMERICA'S LOWEST POINT, BADWATER, AT 282 FEET BELOW SEA LEVEL. LIKE MUCH OF THE GREAT BASIN, THE VALLEY FLOORS ON BOTH SIDES OF THE PANAMINT RANGE HARBORED LARGE FRESHWATER LAKES DURING MOST OF THE ICE AGE. TELESCOPE PEAK HAS A SLIGHT DOUBLE SUMMIT, THE LOWER OF WHICH PROVIDES THE PERFECT LUNCH SPOT. IN THE SHADE OF A PINE, ITS NEEDLES LENDING VOICE TO THE WIND, ONE CAN GAZE AT A PANORAMA THAT STRETCHES FROM MT. WHITNEY IN THE WEST, WHITE MOUNTAIN FAR TO THE NORTH, AND EAST TO CHARLESTON PEAK. AT NIGHT THE LIGHTS OF LAS VEGAS, A HUNDRED MILES AWAY, CAST THE SPRING MOUNTAINS INTO SILHOUETTE. TO THE SOUTH, FAR ACROSS THE MOJAVE DESERT, LOOKING LIKE NOTHING MORE THAN DISTANT CLOUD BANKS ON THE HORIZON, ARE THE SAN BERNADINO AND SAN GABRIEL MOUNTAINS, MADE DISCERNABLE WITH BINOCULARS.
