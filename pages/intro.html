The Seven Laws of the Great View

An Introduction to the Seven Laws

The Seven Laws of the Great View

  • The First Law of the Great View

    • The great view is always a ground-based view.

  • The Second Law of the Great View

    • The great view is enhanced by the method and path used to approach the view.

  • The Third Law of the Great View

    • The great view is enhanced by direct experience with geographical details within the expanse of the view.

  • The Fourth Law of the Great View

    • The great view is enhanced by knowing historical details about places within the expanse of the view.

  • The Fifth Law of the Great View

    • The great view is enhanced by geological knowledge, both structural and temporal, of the landscape within the expanse of the view.

  • The Sixth Law of the Great View

    • An ensemble of great views within sight of one another will enhance each great view.

  • The Seventh Law of the Great View

    • The great view both diminishes and expands the viewer's sense of self.

Appreciating Grand Landscapes

Magnificent landscapes, some of the grandest in the world, exist in the American West. Over the course of my life I have searched for the best viewpoints from which to experience this great scenery. During this time I came to understand certain principles that help to define what makes a scenic view great and how to best appreciate its grandeur. These principles are codified in "The Seven Laws of the Great View," which are the subject of this website. On each of seven pages I explain one of the Seven Laws and give one or two examples from among the many great views of the American West.

I do not believe that I have invented these laws. Rather, I have discovered something universal to the human experience of experiencing and enjoying nature at its grandest and most sublime, and these seven laws merely express ways in which we may enhance our understanding and appreciation of what we are seeing from these majestic overlooks.
